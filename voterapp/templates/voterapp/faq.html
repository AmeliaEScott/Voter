Frequently Asked Questions

Why do you need my email address?

By requiring you to provide your email address, it becomes a little more difficult to vote more than once: When you cast your vote, it won't actually be counted until you click the link sent to your email address.

Of course, if you have more than one valid email address, you could vote more than once, but that seems like a real waste of your time. It's not like this is a real election.

Will you send me spam?

Even if I wanted to, I couldn't. After the initial confirmation email is sent, your email address is irreversibly scrambled using what's called a hashing algorithm. For example, in the database, my email looks like this: rlknyOH9RkmqETXMUQn11T1v_DQxsCF_3A9yuy8sBiPeamcDh8UeQJt3viYR

How did you choose which candidates to put on the ballot?

The candidates on the ballot are all of the candidates from the Democratic or Republican primaries, as well as the third-party candidates who are currently running.

Of course, "currently running" is a bit loosely defined. Pretty much anyone can say they're running for president, so the ballot would be much too long if I included every single person who's running. I tried to limit it to the serious candidates, but that's awfully subjective. If you disagree with my choices, let me know.
