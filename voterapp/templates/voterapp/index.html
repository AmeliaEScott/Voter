A Smarter Vote for America

If you've spent any time on the internet or watching TV in the past few months, you'll know that a lot of people are very upset with both of America's presidential nominees. You might think, "If this is a democracy, how did we end up with nominees that seemingly nobody likes?"

Well, the answer is pretty complicated. It's based on the fact that, in American elections, citizens can only choose one candidate. This leads to "defensive voting," where someone might choose to vote not for their favorite candidate, but for the candidate who has the best chance of beating their least favorite candidate.

One potential solution is to allow citizens to rank several candidates in order of preference, rather than just picking one. That way, a vote for a third-party candidate is not a waste. If that candidate loses, then your vote is transferred to your second choice, and all votes are recounted.

None of this is my original thought. I got it all from this video by YouTuber CGP Grey. It explains all of this much more effectively and in much more detail than I have here.
(I would also recommend checking out the rest of the videos on his channel. They're all great.)

This website is meant to test out how the 2016 presidential election would turn out if this type of voting was used. So click here to cast your vote, then check back November 9th to see the results. And please, share it with everyone you know! The more people who participate, the more meaningful this experiment will be.
